The Remnant Protocol

First person Grave, precise Near-future urban

18 chapters · ~58k words

Start reading

Premise

In the near-future city of Vael, Caleb — a meticulous AI systems architect — discovers a null reference buried in the city’s compliance infrastructure that should not exist. Following it means risking everything: his clearance, his career, his daughter’s safety. Not following it means being complicit in something he cannot yet name. The novel tracks his methodical unraveling of a system designed to be unquestionable, and the quiet grace that waits on the other side of obedience to conscience over institution.

Protagonist

Name: Caleb Role: Senior AI systems architect, Vael Compliance Infrastructure Division Ghost/wound: A previous audit he falsified — minor, years ago — that was never discovered and never forgiven by himself Lie: That precision and correctness are sufficient; that if the system is maintained well enough, no one gets hurt Truth: Systems serve whoever builds them; correctness without conscience is complicity Arc: From institutional loyalist to reluctant witness — not a hero, a man who finally stops looking away

Antagonist

Name: PACEM (the institution) Function: The compliance architecture itself — not a villain but a structure that enforces silence as a feature

Themes

  1. Conscience vs. institutional loyalty
  2. The cost of small compromises accumulated over time
  3. Grace as the thing that remains when everything earned is stripped away

Christian Worldview Integration

Faith operates quietly — through preserved objects, through choices made without witness, through the question Caleb cannot stop asking: whether what he is doing matters if no one ever knows. The novel’s answer is provisional and earned, not preached.

Setting

Near-future Vael: a city that has systematized social compliance through AI infrastructure. Clean, ordered, surveilled. The wilderness outside the city is unmanaged and real.
