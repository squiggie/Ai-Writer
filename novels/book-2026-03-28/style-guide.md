# Style Guide: The Harmonic Deep

## Genre Profile
This novel is **Hard Science Fiction**. It fits because the engine of the story is not a battle, a romance, or a conspiracy reveal, but Mara Vale's encounter with rigorously interpreted evidence: plume chemistry, orbital sampling, signal analysis, extraction economics, and the moral consequences of deciding what counts as life before the data is socially convenient. The book's tension comes from following one scientific premise to its full human and spiritual cost: if Enceladus's ocean is expressing intelligence through harmonics, then every procedural choice aboard *Anselm* becomes a stewardship question, not merely an operational one.

## Comp Authors
- **Ted Chiang**: Gives this novel its model for turning a precise scientific pattern into a moral crisis, so Mara's recognition of the repeating structure feels like a change in reality, not just a clue.
- **Kim Stanley Robinson**: Contributes the systems view this story needs, especially in showing that extraction policy, Earthside need, station hierarchy, and ecology are all part of the same argument Mara is trapped inside.
- **Ursula K. Le Guin**: Contributes the discipline of treating encounter with the truly other as an examination of human assumptions, so the novel keeps asking what Mara's categories cannot yet hold.
- **Andy Weir**: Contributes the pleasure of competent orbital problem-solving, but redirected here away from swagger and toward conscience, where every technical solution also sharpens the ethical stakes.

## POV and Narrative Distance
First person, fixed to Mara Vale, with no exceptions. The narrative distance is close enough that the reader experiences each scene through her trained observational habits before they understand her emotion; she does not report feelings first, she reports readings, anomalies, procedure drift, body response, and only then lets implication accumulate. She cannot narrate anything she did not directly witness, infer from station data, or reasonably reconstruct from mission protocol and conversation.

## Sentence Rhythm
Default rhythm is clear, medium-length, analytical sentences that move the way Mara thinks: observation, qualification, implication. Technical density is welcome when she is working and when the detail changes the stakes. Under pressure, the prose compresses fast into shorter declaratives, stripped comparisons, and operational verbs. That transition marks a precise shift in Mara herself: the moment when analysis stops being professional distance and becomes involuntary moral commitment, especially when the station forces her to act before certainty feels complete.

## Tone and Emotional Register
Primary tone is cerebral wonder under institutional pressure. The reader should feel the loneliness of being early, the dread of watching an extraction machine move faster than proof, and the enlarging awe that comes from realizing the ocean below the ice may be alive in a way human categories barely recognize. Chapter endings should leave the reader with unresolved consequence: a pattern that deepens, a cost that becomes personal, a choice that narrows, or a hint that reverence now requires action.

## Protagonist Voice Signature
Mara's voice is disciplined, exact, and slightly over-controlled. Her sentences often begin from measurement or procedure and only later reveal what the measurement means to her. Her vocabulary register is scientific without showmanship: spectral analysis, plume density, harmonics, calibration drift, orbital lag, extraction tolerance, pressure gradients. Her metaphors come from wave behavior, resonance, instrument error, fracture mechanics, sealed systems, and the hostile physical realities of station life above Enceladus; she should not reach for pastoral, mythic, or poetic imagery unless the scene has pushed her beyond her normal defenses. She never says directly that she is afraid, guilty, grieving, or in awe. Instead, she notices her hand hesitate over a certification field, hears her brother's old engineering cautions embedded in her procedural habits, recalculates a result three times when once would have sufficed, or treats a new data signature with the kind of care usually reserved for the dying. Under maximum pressure, her voice loses subordinate cushioning and becomes almost lab-notebook blunt, which should signal not emotional absence but the point where truth has become action.

## Scene Break Policy
Maximum two scene breaks per chapter, with one preferred. A break is warranted only for a meaningful shift in one of three things: mission-time discontinuity that changes the available evidence, a location change aboard *Anselm* that also changes the scene's social or moral pressure, or deliberate white space after a discovery or betrayal large enough that the reader needs to feel the gap. Do not use breaks to skip difficult transitions between lab work, command conflict, and private reckoning; in this novel those frictions must usually stay on the page.

## What This Novel Is Not
- It is not a military space opera with heroic command theatrics; avoid battle-room rhetoric, fleet-scale spectacle, and action beats that dwarf the quieter terror of certification and stewardship.
- It is not a techno-thriller built on twisty sabotage mechanics alone; avoid treating the harmonic intelligence as merely a puzzle box or the extraction consortium as a cartoon villain apparatus.
- It is not mystical first-contact fantasy in laboratory clothing; avoid language that makes Enceladus's ocean feel magical, telepathic, or conveniently symbolic when the whole force of the novel depends on disciplined evidence.
- It is not generic AI-fiction about "humanity's place in the cosmos" delivered through abstract speeches; avoid floating philosophizing, TED-talk dialogue, and thematic statements untethered from Mara's specific data, guilt, and risk.
- It is not a wisecracking competence fantasy; avoid snark, breezy banter undercutting consequence, or a protagonist who is emotionally transparent and rhetorically polished when this book needs costly restraint.
