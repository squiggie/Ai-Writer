# Protagonist: Dr. Mara Vale

## Core Identity
- **Name**: Dr. Mara Elise Vale
- **Age**: 39
- **Role**: Lead plume analyst, astrobiologist, and spectral systems specialist aboard *Anselm*
- **Education and training**: Astrobiology, signal analysis, cryovolcanic plume chemistry, mission certification procedure

## Physical Description
- Lean from station life rather than athletic vanity; muscle built for restraint, ladders, handholds, and fatigue.
- Dark hair kept short enough to stay out of seals and visors.
- Pale skin gone sallow under orbital lighting and irregular sleep.
- Gray eyes that read as detached until pressure strips away her control.
- A small white scar along her left jaw from an earlier mission accident.
- Usually composed, economical in movement, and visibly irritated by wasted motion or theatrical emotion.

## Ghost, Wound, Lie, Truth

### Ghost
Years earlier, Mara signed incomplete safety data on a mission where her younger brother served as an engineer. She told herself the uncertainty was operationally tolerable and that delay would cost too much. The resulting systems failure killed him. She lives with the fact that she did not falsify the numbers, but she did authorize action before truth was secure.

### Wound
Mara no longer trusts her own moral intuition when evidence is incomplete. She believes any early act of conviction risks becoming one more lethal self-deception. Her grief hardened into procedural absolutism.

### Lie
"Only certainty gives you the right to act."

Secondary forms of the lie:
- If the data is not complete, restraint is innocence.
- Naming value too early contaminates science.
- Duty is fulfilled by precision alone.
- Human need automatically outranks any ambiguous nonhuman claim.

### Truth
"When harm is measurable, uncertainty does not excuse inaction."

Full truth arc:
- Scientific rigor is necessary but not morally sufficient.
- Evidence creates obligation before consensus arrives.
- Creation is not valuable only when it is useful to humans.
- Witness may cost reputation, access, and control, but silence also acts.
- Courage is not the opposite of precision; it is what keeps precision from becoming a shield for fear.

## Motivation
- Surface goal at story start: complete extraction certification correctly, avoid interpretive overreach, and protect the integrity of the data.
- Hidden goal: never again be the person who acts too soon and kills someone because she wanted to believe a risk was acceptable.
- Deep need: accept that refusing to act is also a decision, and that truth sometimes has to be defended before it is institutionally safe.

## Voice Signature

### Sentence Patterns
- Begins from observation before interpretation: "The ratio repeated before I had a theory for it."
- Uses qualification as emotional armor: "probably," "within tolerance," "not yet enough," "statistically ugly."
- Thinks in chained logic: observation, disproof attempt, narrowed implication.
- Under stress, sentences shorten and lose cushioning.
- Rarely names emotion directly; bodily reaction and procedural fixation carry the feeling.

### Vocabulary
- Natural lexicon includes harmonics, calibration, drift, aliasing, lattice, plume density, thresholds, vectors, pressure gradients, certification, contamination, load, response time.
- Avoids inflated poetic language unless awe breaks through her usual control.
- Metaphors come from resonance, fracture, sealed systems, and instrument error.

### What She Notices
- Instrument behavior before room mood.
- Timing delays, hand hesitation, breath changes, and small failures of routine.
- The way hardware reveals institutional intent.
- When language gets softened to make harm administratively tolerable.
- Physical signs of stress in others only after they cross into operational consequence.

## Relationships at Story Start
- **Director Ivo Serran**: Respect mixed with caution. He is intelligent, controlled, and politically fluent in ways Mara distrusts, but she initially believes he can still be persuaded by evidence.
- **Linh Ekwueme**: Closest working ally. Mara trusts Linh's systems instincts and candor more than she admits aloud.
- **Pavel Ibarra**: Professional peer with real intellectual credibility. Mara expects rigor from him and is stung when his ethics diverge from hers.
- **Chaplain Tomas Orlov**: Anomalous presence she half-avoids, because he asks moral questions she cannot reduce to instrumentation.
- **Her dead brother, Elias Vale**: Not present physically, but functionally her most influential relationship. His habits and warnings live inside her procedure.

## Internal State at Chapter 1
- Exhausted, hyper-competent, and emotionally over-regulated.
- Proud of being the person who does not overstate evidence.
- Quietly terrified of repeating her old failure in either direction.
- Still believes the safest moral posture is analytical restraint.
- Not open to religious language, but already vulnerable to the idea that discovery might carry obligation.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Arc Summary
- **Chapter 1**: Treats the anomaly as probable drift and defaults to containment.
- **Chapter 2**: Begins disproving alternatives and resists Orlov's moral framing.
- **Chapter 3**: Learns evidence will be judged inside a production deadline.
- **Chapter 4**: Feels first real awe when the pattern behaves too elegantly for noise.
- **Chapter 5**: Her brother's death becomes directly entangled with the certification question.
- **Chapter 6**: Risks proximity and action to get better data.
- **Chapter 7**: Understands the human-benefit logic aligned against her.
- **Chapter 8**: Crosses from anomaly science into contact shock.
- **Chapter 9**: Gains provisional allies, but not enough cover to feel safe.
- **Chapter 10**: Decides obligation may begin before consensus.
- **Chapter 11**: Achieves certainty and discovers certainty does not remove danger.
- **Chapter 12**: Experiences institutional silencing and loses faith in process neutrality.
- **Chapter 13**: Refuses the compromise script at personal cost.
- **Chapter 14**: Sees harm become concrete and can no longer hide inside abstraction.
- **Chapter 15**: Endures isolation when even allies choose utilitarian triage.
- **Chapter 16**: Acts in open defiance by transmitting the truth.
- **Chapter 17**: States aloud the principle she has finally accepted: uncertainty is not innocence.
- **Chapter 18**: Reorients from exposure alone to protection by finding a workable shield.
- **Chapter 19**: Publicly confesses both the evidence and the wound beneath her obsession with certainty.
- **Chapter 20**: Participates in costly rescue rather than detached analysis.
- **Chapter 21**: Returns changed; sees the intelligence as vulnerable neighbor, not finding.
- **Chapter 22**: Embraces witness as a long-term vocation, even without reward or absolution.

## End State
- Still rigorous, still scientifically exacting, but no longer hiding inside exactness.
- Willing to bear blame, uncertainty, and loss if stewardship requires it.
- Sees science as answerability to reality, not merely control over it.
