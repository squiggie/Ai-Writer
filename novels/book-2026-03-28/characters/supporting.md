# Supporting Characters

## Linh Ekwueme
- **Role**: Systems engineer and acoustic modeling specialist; Mara's primary ally inside the science-and-engineering chain
- **Function in story**: Helps convert anomaly into defensible evidence, shares risk during the skiff pass and data leak, and becomes a practical bridge between theory and action
- **Voice note**: Crisp, unsentimental, dry without being flippant. Speaks in exact engineering judgments and hates inflated language.
- **Relationship to Mara**: Closest living peer. Mutual respect built on competence, argument, and earned trust rather than overt emotional confession.

## Pavel Ibarra
- **Role**: Biochemist and validation-minded scientific peer
- **Function in story**: Serves as the credible ally who will not cross certain lines until the evidence is overwhelming, then later fractures from Mara over utilitarian cost
- **Voice note**: Careful, intellectually fair, slightly formal. He sounds like someone always editing a paper in his head.
- **Relationship to Mara**: Professional equal whose respect matters. Their break hurts because he represents the last version of scientific caution Mara used to inhabit.

## Chaplain Tomas Orlov
- **Role**: Aging flight chaplain and maintenance-capable pastoral presence aboard *Anselm*
- **Function in story**: Introduces moral language without coercion, helps Mara name obligation, and later assists her while detained
- **Voice note**: Spare, patient, concrete. He does not sermonize; he places a sentence gently and lets it remain.
- **Relationship to Mara**: A quiet irritant at first, then a moral witness she comes to trust because he never exploits her uncertainty.

## Nadiya Quist
- **Role**: Security chief responsible for enforcement, access control, and detention protocol
- **Function in story**: Gives the institutional response a human face and keeps the suppression apparatus credible without cartoon brutality
- **Voice note**: Short, operational, nearly affectless. She speaks in permissions, risks, and containment language.
- **Relationship to Mara**: Respectful but adversarial once command classifies the findings. Quist is not personally cruel, which makes her obedience more unsettling.

## Sefa Moran
- **Role**: Senior propulsion officer and orbital mechanics lead
- **Function in story**: Critical in evaluating and eventually executing the shielding maneuver that alters extraction geometry
- **Voice note**: Fast, mathematically intuitive, impatient with moral vagueness until the engineering case becomes undeniable
- **Relationship to Mara**: Distant for most of the book, then briefly crucial when her evidence has to become a burn solution instead of a report.

## Hana Bisset
- **Role**: Medical officer overseeing trauma response and crew health
- **Function in story**: Grounds the human cost of both extraction delay and extraction damage; present during the injured-technician sequence
- **Voice note**: Plainspoken, tired, compassionate under compression
- **Relationship to Mara**: Cordial but limited until crisis. Her perspective reminds Mara that "human cost" is never abstract aboard the station either.

## Elias Vale
- **Role**: Mara's deceased younger brother, former mission engineer
- **Function in story**: The defining absent presence shaping Mara's fear of acting without certainty
- **Voice note**: Known only through remembered cautions, habits, and fragments of older mission language; practical, wry, attentive to failure chains
- **Relationship to Mara**: The lost person around whom her internal life still orbits. He is memory, wound, accusation, and in some ways the standard of honest craft she still wants to meet.

## Injured Technician: Jo Rainer
- **Role**: Mid-level extraction technician hurt during the destabilization event
- **Function in story**: Makes the emergency physically immediate and keeps the conflict from collapsing into "alien life versus faceless human need"
- **Voice note**: Workmanlike, direct, not philosophically inclined
- **Relationship to Mara**: Minimal before the accident. Afterward, Jo's injury sharpens Mara's understanding that the same extraction logic is endangering both the station crew and the ocean below.
