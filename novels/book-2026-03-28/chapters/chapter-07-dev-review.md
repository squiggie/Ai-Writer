## Developmental Review: Chapter 7

### Structural Assessment
The chapter is structurally coherent and clearly delivers the Chapter 7 function from the outline: Mara is forced to hear the institutional case for extraction in its strongest form, and the ending lands on her recognition that truth alone will not move this system. The single-scene construction also fits the chapter's purpose. Keeping the procurement meeting continuous creates pressure and prevents the chapter from diffusing its moral argument with unnecessary cuts.

The main structural weakness is pacing compression inside the meeting. The chapter gives substantial space to policy framing, actuarial context, and argument restatement, but the emotional hinge points do not always receive proportionally more room than the procedural material. The mortality-display reveal, Nadir's profit-oriented question, and Serran's preloaded ruling are the moments that should feel like the floor dropping out beneath Mara; all three land, but each could slow further in Mara's physical and perceptual experience so the chapter's emotional weight distinctly exceeds its briefing weight. Relatedly, the chapter closes on strong recognition, but not yet on a fully sharpened decision impulse. Since this beat sits in the "Go" phase, it would benefit from a slightly stronger handoff into Mara's next active move, even if the action itself belongs to Chapter 8.

Scene-to-scene transition is handled cleanly without relying on scene breaks. There are no unjustified breaks here, and the movement from lab to annex is written on the page rather than skipped. That matches the style guide well.

### Character Arc Status
Mara is in the correct arc position. The chapter advances her from "I have dangerous evidence" to "I now understand the governing logic that will be used against that evidence." That is an important escalation, and it deepens her conflict with Serran beyond simple bad-faith obstruction. The strongest character work is that Mara is not allowed to dismiss the Earthside need as fake; she has to absorb its reality and still recognize the moral failure in the framework.

Her wound from *Khepri* is present in the right amount. The brief Elias/load-path recall gives the meeting historical and personal pressure without turning the chapter into a flashback. Mara also remains recognizably herself: disciplined, analytic, withholding direct feeling, and translating emotion through measurement, classification, and system language.

The gap is embodiment at peak stress. The chapter includes some good body markers, especially the sample-case grip, the held hands, the neck tendons, and the scraped breath, but several major turns still resolve primarily as intellectual interpretation. Because this novel's voice depends on Mara registering emotion through procedure and body response rather than abstract declaration, the key reversals would be stronger if her body were more continuously present as the argument closes around her.

### World-Building Notes
The world-building is strong and internally consistent. Procurement culture feels distinct from science-deck culture, the mortality model makes the extraction mission's human rationale concrete, and the legal language around biosignature thresholds and ambiguity reads like plausible institutional machinery rather than villain exposition. Nadir is particularly useful because she widens the system beyond Serran and shows that the pressure is infrastructural, not merely personal.

The science-to-policy interface also stays grounded. Mara's particulate findings are translated into operational and legal terms in a believable way, and the chapter continues the novel's strength of making scientific ambiguity carry real procedural consequence.

One caution: the room occasionally explains its governing logic so fully that it risks sounding slightly too self-articulating. Institutions often reveal themselves through selective phrasing, omission, and euphemism. A small reduction in the explicitness of some argument loops could make the scene feel even more dangerous and realistic while preserving clarity.

### Thematic Resonance
This chapter lands the novel's central thematic conflict well: stewardship versus utilitarian extraction under conditions of uncertainty. It also handles the Christian-worldview-adjacent moral terrain with restraint. The chapter does not sermonize; instead it dramatizes how a human system can make the uncounted morally invisible by refusing to classify them. That is one of the strongest thematic moves in the draft.

There is also good moral complexity in the fact that Serran is not presented as cartoonishly cruel. He believes he is carrying a real burden, which gives the conflict the right shape for this book. The danger is not obvious greed but a ledger that can only recognize lives after power agrees on the category.

The strongest thematic line is not any single speech but the cumulative realization that "admissibility" has displaced truth. That said, a few lines edge toward polished thematic summation rather than Mara's lived cognition in the moment. The chapter will land harder if its biggest ideas stay just slightly more embedded in her sensory and procedural noticing.

### Bestseller Compliance
The chapter opens with immediate tension, orients the reader quickly, and sustains conflict without dead air. The ending generates forward pull because Mara now understands the institutional argument she must beat, which creates genuine "one more chapter" momentum.

Emotional rhythm is good but not yet optimal. The chapter maintains pressure, but the internal texture is somewhat uniform through the long central exchange. The result is that the chapter reads as consistently strong rather than sharply escalating. To hit the intended pacing pattern, procedural explanation should move faster and the decisive emotional reversals should slow more.

The voice is largely consistent with the style guide: first-person fixed, analytic, technically literate, and emotionally over-controlled. Where it drifts is in a few aphoristic or essay-like sentences that feel more authorially concluded than Mara-narrated under pressure. Those moments are not numerous, but they are noticeable because the rest of the chapter is so well aligned to voice.

### Strengths
- The chapter fulfills the outline beat precisely by forcing Mara to confront the strongest utilitarian case for extraction rather than an easy straw man.
- Procurement Annex B is a vivid setting extension of the station's power structure and helps the world feel larger than the lab-command binary.
- Serran's argument is credible, morally serious, and therefore genuinely threatening.
- Mara's voice is mostly well controlled, technical, and specific to her training.
- The chapter avoids unjustified scene breaks and writes the transition into the meeting on the page.
- The ending reframes the conflict from evidence-gathering to ledger-fighting, which is a meaningful escalation.

### Concerns
- Pacing is too even across procedural explanation and emotional rupture; the chapter's biggest turns need more slowdown than the policy discussion surrounding them.
- Mara's body is present intermittently, but not enough in several high-pressure beats where the prose shifts back toward abstract interpretation.
- Dialogue in the central meeting becomes information-dense enough that some exchanges read as argument transfer more than physically inhabited confrontation.
- A few thematic lines feel slightly too polished or aphoristic for Mara's close, pressure-compressed narration.
- The chapter ends on realization rather than a more energized decision vector, which slightly softens the propulsion into Chapter 8.

### Suggestions
- Slow three moments in particular: the first sight of the mortality model, Nadir's question about whether disruption could improve yield, and Serran's formal ruling. Give each of those turns a few more beats of Mara's physical processing so the emotional weight clearly outruns the procedural frame.
- Trim or compress some of the repeated institutional argument so the meeting escalates instead of feeling evenly distributed across similar rhetorical exchanges.
- Add more physical beats inside dialogue-heavy sections: how Serran uses the room, what Nadir does before asking the profit question, how Mara's posture, breath, jaw, hands, or vestibular sense change as the argument corners her.
- Rephrase the most essay-like lines into observations Mara would naturally make under pressure through system language, sensory detail, or procedural inference rather than near-maxim statements.
- Consider sharpening the final paragraph so Mara's recognition more explicitly seeds the next chapter's action logic, even if the chapter still ends in comprehension rather than overt defiance.
