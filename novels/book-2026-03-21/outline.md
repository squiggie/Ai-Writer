# The Bell of Ash Fen — Outline

## Structure
Chosen structure: Freytag's Pyramid
Chapter count: 22
Rationale: This novel is a pressure-cooker historical drama where institutional power, famine logistics, and private guilt converge into one public moral decision. Freytag's Pyramid best fits that shape: a long rising action can carry accumulating legal, social, and spiritual pressure; a concentrated climax can center Elin's public witness; and a meaningful falling action/denouement can show consequence, mercy, and repair rather than ending at the moment of exposure. Twenty-two chapters gives enough room for procedural texture (records, grain routes, oath politics) and emotional turns without diffusing urgency.

## Chapter Outline

### Chapter 1: Bell Before Dawn
- Beat: Exposition - World, voice, and pressure baseline
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Late October 1539, predawn
- Opening: Elin climbs the priory bell stair in freezing mist to count sacks in the lower granary before matins.
- Core scene: She discovers the stores are already short for winter while a messenger brings notice that royal commissioners will arrive within a fortnight.
- Emotional beat: Quiet dread tightening into duty.
- Ending: She is ordered to prepare the seizure inventory herself.
- Stakes: If her figures are wrong, the priory and villages lose legal standing and food.

### Chapter 2: Ash on the Register
- Beat: Exposition - Ghost and lie established
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Same day, midday
- Opening: Elin recopies a damaged oath ledger and pauses at the crossed-out line bearing her dead husband's name.
- Core scene: Prior Anselm reminds her survival depends on accuracy and restraint; Elin privately resolves to keep her head down and protect stores quietly.
- Emotional beat: Shame bound to control.
- Ending: A villager asks for alms grain, and Elin must refuse by rule.
- Stakes: Her silence preserves order in the short term but deepens village hunger.

### Chapter 3: The Receiver Arrives
- Beat: Exposition - Antagonist introduced
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Three days later
- Opening: Hooves and iron-shod carts break the cloister hush as Master Rafe Quill enters with clerks and armed men.
- Core scene: Quill publicly praises reform, then privately demands full custody of relic and alms records, appointing Elin to report directly to him.
- Emotional beat: Polite menace.
- Ending: Quill keeps the archive keys and leaves Elin one hour nightly to copy ledgers.
- Stakes: Evidence can disappear at his discretion.

### Chapter 4: The Sealed Roll
- Beat: Exposition -> Rising Action trigger (inciting incident)
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: That night
- Opening: By rushlight in the damp archive, Elin lifts a warped chest panel to recover mildew-stained account rolls.
- Core scene: She finds a sealed packet of alms transfers that do not match the official priory books and include Quill's countersign.
- Emotional beat: Shock edged with forbidden hope.
- Ending: Footsteps stop outside the archive door as she hides the packet in her sleeve.
- Stakes: Possessing the roll could expose fraud or get her hanged for concealment.

### Chapter 5: Measures That Do Not Balance
- Beat: Rising Action - First investigation
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Early November 1539
- Opening: Elin weighs grain from three barns at first light while pretending to conduct routine spoilage checks.
- Core scene: The physical stores confirm large alms withdrawals were recorded as parish relief but never delivered.
- Emotional beat: Certainty replacing suspicion.
- Ending: She learns Quill's private barges moved grain south two nights earlier.
- Stakes: Fraud is active, not historical; delay means more hunger.

### Chapter 6: Counsel in the Choir
- Beat: Rising Action - First moral test
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Same week, evening
- Opening: During compline, Elin cannot follow the psalm because she is counting false entries in her head.
- Core scene: She confides in Prior Anselm, who urges patience until lawful channels open; Elin hears it as another command to stay silent.
- Emotional beat: Frustrated isolation.
- Ending: She agrees to wait seven days for a formal hearing that may never come.
- Stakes: Waiting may protect the priory legally but costs the villages immediate relief.

### Chapter 7: Reed-Road Market
- Beat: Rising Action - External stakes widened
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: One week later
- Opening: Elin crosses a flooded reed-road to market, where priory grain is sold at triple winter rate under private marks.
- Core scene: She traces the marks to Quill's factors and witnesses mothers trading tools for half-measures of spoiled barley.
- Emotional beat: Moral outrage breaking through numbness.
- Ending: A factor recognizes her priory seal-ring and sends word ahead.
- Stakes: Quill now knows she is asking the wrong questions.

### Chapter 8: The Informer's Debt
- Beat: Rising Action - Complication and distrust
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Two days later
- Opening: Elin returns to find Matthew Cale, a former informer she despises, waiting by the scriptorium door.
- Core scene: Matthew admits he once passed names to officials, but offers to show her where duplicate shipping tallies are hidden if she protects his sister.
- Emotional beat: Revulsion mixed with reluctant need.
- Ending: Elin accepts the lead but refuses him absolution.
- Stakes: Her best source is morally compromised and socially dangerous.

### Chapter 9: Keys and Chains
- Beat: Rising Action - Antagonist countermove
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Mid-November 1539
- Opening: Quill convenes a formal inventory session and smiles as he replaces all archive locks in front of witnesses.
- Core scene: He assigns armed oversight to Elin's worktable and requires each copied page be sealed nightly under his mark.
- Emotional beat: Claustrophobia under surveillance.
- Ending: Elin discovers her hidden sealed roll is gone from its hiding place.
- Stakes: Without proof, accusation becomes treasonous slander.

### Chapter 10: Winter Tithe
- Beat: Rising Action - Cost paid by allies
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Late November 1539
- Opening: A bell for burial tolls while sleet drives through the priory yard.
- Core scene: Village reeve Tomas, who challenged Quill's rates, is arrested for "incitement," and his household loses ration priority.
- Emotional beat: Helpless guilt.
- Ending: Tomas's daughter begs Elin for grain she no longer controls.
- Stakes: Resistance now draws direct punishment on families.

### Chapter 11: Midpoint Ledger
- Beat: Rising Action - Midpoint revelation and commitment
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: First week of December 1539
- Opening: Guided by Matthew through a drained eel trench at low tide, Elin reaches an abandoned weigh house.
- Core scene: She finds duplicate ledgers and a receipt trail tying Quill to a senior reform office, proving deliberate alms diversion as policy, not theft by clerks.
- Emotional beat: Horror at the scale, clarity of purpose.
- Ending: Elin swears she will bring the documents to public record, not private petition.
- Stakes: She shifts from passive preservation to active exposure.

### Chapter 12: Terms of Submission
- Beat: Rising Action - Pressure before confrontation
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Two days later
- Opening: Quill summons Elin to the chapter house and sets a prepared inventory for her signature.
- Core scene: He offers protection for her and the priory poor if she certifies the false books and denounces "forged rumors."
- Emotional beat: Temptation wrapped in terror.
- Ending: She asks for one night to consider, and Quill grants it too easily.
- Stakes: Signing secures short-term safety but legalizes ongoing famine profiteering.

### Chapter 13: Candle in the Loft
- Beat: Rising Action - Pinch toward abyss
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: That night
- Opening: Elin climbs to the bell loft to hide the copied evidence inside a cracked beam above the rope drum.
- Core scene: Matthew arrives wounded, warning that Quill plans a staged food riot to justify seizures and arrests.
- Emotional beat: Panic sharpened into urgency.
- Ending: Smoke rises from the far tithe barn before dawn.
- Stakes: Violence will erase records and let Quill recast victims as criminals.

### Chapter 14: The False Signature
- Beat: Rising Action - Abyss choice (temporary surrender)
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Mid-December 1539
- Opening: In the commissioners' chamber, ink freezes in the inkwell as Elin is handed the false schedule.
- Core scene: Seeing soldiers holding villagers in the yard, she signs to buy time and prevent immediate bloodshed.
- Emotional beat: Self-disgust and grief.
- Ending: Quill announces the priory's remaining relief grain will be transferred at once.
- Stakes: Her silence now materially harms the people she meant to protect.

### Chapter 15: Bread Line at Vespers
- Beat: Rising Action - Consequence forcing transformation
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Three days later
- Opening: Elin counts empty baskets outside the priory gate while bells call evening prayer over a crowd that has not eaten.
- Core scene: A child dies in the queue after the transfer convoy fails to return; villagers blame the priory and each other.
- Emotional beat: Breaking point.
- Ending: Elin confesses to Prior Anselm that she signed the false inventory.
- Stakes: Her lie has become lethal; inaction is no longer morally possible.

### Chapter 16: Confession and Charge
- Beat: Rising Action -> Climax threshold (transformation)
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Night of Chapter 15
- Opening: In the darkened chapel, Elin kneels beside the cold stone where her husband once stood for oath judgment.
- Core scene: She confesses fully, receives a hard charge to tell truth with mercy, and chooses to protect Matthew despite his past informing.
- Emotional beat: Sorrow turning to resolve.
- Ending: She sends word to parish elders to gather at morning bell.
- Stakes: Public action will expose her perjury and invite retaliation.

### Chapter 17: Bell of Witness
- Beat: Climax - Public exposure
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Next morning, winter dawn
- Opening: Instead of matins, Elin rings the great bell out of order, summoning villagers and officials into one yard.
- Core scene: Before commissioners, she reads the duplicate rolls, names transfer routes, and presents signatures linking Quill's network to alms diversion.
- Emotional beat: Fear braided with fierce clarity.
- Ending: Quill calls her traitor and orders seizure of the records as the crowd surges.
- Stakes: Truth is now public, but control of force still belongs to Quill.

### Chapter 18: Fire in the Archive
- Beat: Climax aftermath / Falling Action - Immediate fallout
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Same day, later morning
- Opening: As shouting turns to blows, someone torches the outer archive room.
- Core scene: Elin and Matthew drag ledger chests through smoke while Prior Anselm brokers a temporary stand-down with parish men.
- Emotional beat: Terror and grim cooperation.
- Ending: Quill escapes by barge with two clerks and one lockbox.
- Stakes: Crucial evidence survives, but the chief architect of the scheme is still at large.

### Chapter 19: Mercy at the Gate
- Beat: Falling Action - Thematic test (justice with mercy)
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Two days later
- Opening: Villagers gather with cudgels to lynch Matthew for his earlier informing.
- Core scene: Elin steps between them, publicly names his wrongdoing and his restitution, and demands due process rather than revenge.
- Emotional beat: Costly compassion under pressure.
- Ending: The crowd disperses, but Elin loses standing with former allies who wanted blood.
- Stakes: Choosing mercy risks social fracture and her remaining influence.

### Chapter 20: Hearing at Ely Road
- Beat: Falling Action - Institutional reckoning
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Early January 1540
- Opening: Elin rides in freezing rain to a regional hearing carrying smoke-scarred ledgers wrapped in waxed cloth.
- Core scene: Under hostile questioning, she admits her false signature, then establishes the fraud chain with corroborated tallies from multiple parishes.
- Emotional beat: Vulnerable honesty.
- Ending: An interim writ freezes Quill's grain contracts pending crown review.
- Stakes: Partial legal victory buys time, but final judgment could still bury the case.

### Chapter 21: Stores Reopened
- Beat: Denouement - Material repair begins
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Late January 1540
- Opening: Priory doors open at dawn to supervised ration lines under parish witnesses from three villages.
- Core scene: Confiscated stores are redistributed by recorded household need, and Elin oversees a restitution ledger that includes names previously erased.
- Emotional beat: Fragile relief.
- Ending: News arrives that Ash Fen Priory will still be dissolved by summer decree.
- Stakes: Justice in one case does not stop the wider dismantling of their world.

### Chapter 22: After the Bell
- Beat: Denouement - Return changed
- POV: Elin Marris
- Timeline: Candlemas, February 1540
- Opening: On Candlemas morning, Elin writes a new village register in a rented loft after the priory's scriptorium is sealed.
- Core scene: She records her husband's name without erasure, appoints Matthew as witness-clerk under supervision, and commits the grain and alms accounts to communal custody.
- Emotional beat: Earned hope without illusion.
- Ending: The bell rings from a stripped tower as Elin closes the ledger on an open question: what must be preserved next.
- Stakes: The old institution is ending, but a truthful, merciful civic memory can survive if she keeps choosing courage.
