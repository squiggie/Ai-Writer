## Developmental Review: Chapter 2

### Structural Assessment
Chapter 2 cleanly executes its Exposition function in the Freytag setup phase: it foregrounds the wound (Jon Marris's struck name), clarifies the operating pressure (inventory legality versus hunger), and lands on a costly rule-bound refusal that turns abstract scarcity into a specific human consequence. Scene-sequel logic is mostly strong: Elin's goal is to preserve institutional standing through defensible records; conflict arrives through time pressure, rumor, and relief demand; disaster is moral rather than logistical, as correct process still harms a sick child. The chapter's architecture is coherent and cumulative.

Pacing is effective at the macro level (slow procedural pressure escalating to an emotionally charged gate decision), but the interior rhythm is somewhat too even in the middle third. Procedural reconciliation passages carry high authenticity, yet they can flatten contrast if they consume the same sentence density as the two key emotional nodes (husband-name copy; Agnes refusal). The chapter would benefit from sharper compression in routine accounting beats so the emotional high-cost moments can slow even further and breathe.

### Character Arc Status
Elin's ghost/wound is clearly present and active: the crossed-out entry for Jon binds her present caution to prior harm caused by strict factual testimony. Her operating lie is established with precision: control, restraint, and perfect records can contain damage. The chapter also plants the arc tension effectively by showing that this strategy preserves legal footing while failing immediate human need.

Her decision behavior is consistent with Chapter 1 continuity and with her voice signature (inventory-first cognition, procedural framing of feeling). The end-state of this chapter positions her correctly for later transformation: she has not chosen public truth yet, but the moral cost of silence is now personalized and documented.

### World-Building Notes
Institutional mechanics are a major strength here. Chain-of-custody details, witness protocols, seal procedure, ration offsets, and petition handling feel materially grounded and narratively functional. The world serves story pressure rather than ornamental atmosphere.

Continuity is strong overall with `memory.md` (14-day order pressure, custody protocol, active fever pressure), but one numeric phrasing needs clarification to avoid ledger inconsistency downstream: "Forty-nine to forty-one. Nine half to eight. Twelve rye to ten." The wheat-half line is ambiguous as written and risks conflicting with the established shortage thread if later chapters quote exact figures.

### Thematic Resonance
The chapter lands its core theme well: truth, law, and mercy are not cleanly aligned under corrupt pressure. Christian worldview elements are integrated through action and consequence (care for the poor, lawful witness, refusal of panic speech) rather than didactic dialogue. Moral clarity remains intact without flattening complexity.

The strongest thematic move is the denied wheat petition paired with practical mercy alternatives (healer, broth, morning priority). This avoids caricature and keeps the ethical conflict costly on all sides.

### Bestseller Compliance
The opening has immediate texture and tension (damaged oath ledger, compromised records, personal name trigger) and sustains investment through concrete stakes. Chapter ending generates forward pull with unresolved operational and moral pressure. Emotional beat cadence is appropriate for an exposition chapter.

Primary compliance risk is not hook/ending, but pacing contrast inside the chapter: procedural blocks occasionally read at equal intensity to emotional pivots. Tightening that contrast will improve compulsive readability while preserving historical specificity.

### Strengths
- Ghost and lie are established on-page with strong symbolic and procedural integration.
- Voice is largely consistent with `style-guide.md`: close third limited, ledger-minded cognition, material/legal diction, restrained emotional register.
- Dialogue scenes are generally anchored by physical action in space (coughing, seals, mud, rain, child's breathing), not pure exposition.
- No scene-break overuse; temporal/location transitions are written rather than outsourced to `---`.
- Chapter ending is high-friction and thematically aligned, with clear unresolved consequence.

### Concerns
- Middle procedural sections are slightly overextended, reducing pace contrast between routine operations and key emotional beats.
- Embodied response in two pivotal moments can go deeper: Elin at Jon's struck line, and Elin during/after Agnes's direct accusation.
- A few diction choices trend modern relative to the period-conscious voice standard (for example, phrasing like "cover" and "data first" cadence).
- The alms-variance numeric line is ambiguous and may create continuity drift in later ledger references.
- Several dialogue runs (especially in the chapter-house rule setting and gate refusal exchange) could use one additional physical beat each to keep subtext active under information-heavy lines.

### Suggestions
- Compress repetitive reconciliation/accounting narration in the middle third by roughly 10-15%, then reallocate that space to slower emotional processing at the husband-name beat and Agnes refusal aftermath.
- Add 2-3 concrete body cues for Elin at emotional inflection points (breath, throat, stomach, hand tremor recovery) so feeling is carried physically, not mainly through cognitive procedure.
- Insert brief physical micro-beats inside longer dialogue runs (eye-line shifts, posture changes, handling of objects, weather contact) to prevent informational stacking.
- Normalize potentially modern diction into period-fitting equivalents while preserving readability and precision.
- Clarify the variance line in unambiguous ledger terms consistent with established continuity, so later chapters can cite exact shortages without reinterpretation.
- Add one concise private resolve beat after Anselm's restraint directive that explicitly frames Elin's current strategy as deliberate short-term silence to protect stores until evidentiary footing is stronger.
