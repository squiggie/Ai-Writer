## Developmental Review: Chapter 6

### Structural Assessment
This chapter executes the Chapter 6 beat from the outline with high precision: Elin enters compline fragmented by evidence arithmetic, confides in Anselm and Stephen, is pressed to wait for lawful process, and ends by agreeing to hold line for seven days under protest. In Freytag terms, it is effective Rising Action moral compression rather than plot-sprawl.

Scene-sequel flow is strong and explicit:
- Goal: secure immediate action against ongoing diversion.
- Conflict: legal strategy, custody vulnerability, and institutional fragility under Quill's control.
- Disaster/constraint: no immediate lawful mechanism faster than seven days.
- Reaction: anger, grief-memory, procedural distrust.
- Dilemma: act alone now and likely be neutralized, or submit to delayed lawful channel while harm continues.
- Decision: hold line seven days and formalize multi-copy custody at dawn.

Pacing largely matches emotional weight. Key moral argument scenes are allowed to breathe, while procedural setup (documents, witness chain, dispatch packet preparation) moves efficiently. The chapter is long, but it earns length by building a single decision under layered pressure. No scene breaks are used, and that choice works: transitions are written on-page and do not rely on white-space substitution.

### Character Arc Status
Elin advances from investigator to constrained strategist. The chapter keeps her wound/lie pattern intact: she mistrusts patience as a mask for abandonment, and she hears lawful caution through the memory of her husband's failed process. Her agreement is not surrender but costly tactical restraint.

Arc movement is visible and earned:
- She discloses concealed attestation sheets (risking self-incrimination).
- She argues for immediate interdiction on moral grounds.
- She accepts procedural containment only after securing evidentiary durability (three copies, distributed custody).

This is a meaningful midpoint in her transformation toward public witness: she is no longer passive, but she is not yet fully public or irreversible.

### World-Building Notes
World logic and institutional mechanics remain consistent with prior chapters:
- Receiver custody pressure and legal-channel constraints are coherent.
- Ecclesial schedule (compline/lauds), dispatch practice, seal usage, and witness procedure feel period-grounded.
- Flood/tide logistics continue to function as both setting and plot engine.

No continuity breaks detected against `memory.md` threads (missing sacks, south-service extraction, Hobb/Tomas corroboration, hidden attestation sheets, infirmary pressure).

### Thematic Resonance
The chapter lands the novel's core thematic tension cleanly: truth versus procedural survival, and justice versus timing. Christian worldview integration remains organic through confession language, mercy/legal witness framing, and costly moral choice rather than sermonizing.

The final movement preserves the novel's tonal target from `style-guide.md`: tightening dread plus costly hope. Elin's closing uncertainty (counsel or surrender) is thematically on-point and emotionally honest.

### Bestseller Compliance
- Opening starts in motion and internal conflict.
- Stakes are both immediate (infirmary hunger, next ebb) and systemic (legal custody, seizure authority).
- Chapter ending creates strong forward pull.
- Emotional beats occur at sustained intervals and are given page-time.

Craft checklist targets called out for this review are mostly met:
- Pacing aligns to emotional weight.
- Body is present in emotional scenes (hands, breath, muscle tension, heat, fatigue, tactile detail).
- Dialogue includes physical beats and subtext actions, not only information transfer.
- Scene-break policy is respected; written transitions carry movement.
- Voice strongly matches style guide (inventory cognition, legal/ecclesial diction, marsh/ledger metaphor system).

### Strengths
- Excellent single-scene pressure architecture with clear moral stakes.
- Strong embodiment during high-emotion exchanges; Elin's physical state tracks her inner conflict.
- Dialogue is dynamic and grounded in action beats (coughing, handling documents, posture shifts, sealing workflow).
- Procedural detail serves tension instead of flattening it.
- Voice fidelity is high and consistent with chapter-to-chapter trajectory.

### Concerns
- Mid-chapter argument loop repeats a few legal-risk points that are already established; this slightly blunts escalation.
- Under maximum pressure, some paragraphs remain syntactically dense where the style guide suggests more clipped declarative rhythm.
- Peter/maud urgency is invoked effectively but briefly; one additional concrete sensory callback could sharpen the embodied cost of waiting.

### Suggestions
- Trim 2-3 redundant exchange beats in the central debate to increase perceived escalation without reducing complexity.
- In one late high-pressure section, convert a few long constructions into shorter declarative lines to better mark pressure-state rhythm.
- Add one brief sensory memory beat (for example Peter's breathing, infirmary smell, or triage texture) right before Elin's final decision to intensify bodily stakes.
