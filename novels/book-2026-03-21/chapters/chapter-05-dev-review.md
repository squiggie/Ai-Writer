## Developmental Review: Chapter 5

### Structural Assessment
This chapter lands the Chapter 5 outline beat cleanly: investigation moves from suspicion to operational confirmation, and the ending delivers active-fraud urgency with a concrete next-window threat. Structurally, it functions as strong Rising Action in Freytag terms.

Scene architecture is mostly solid:
- Scene 1 goal: conduct lawful spoilage survey while testing whether declared losses match physical reality.
- Scene 1 conflict: surveillance (Bate/Harwood), constrained access, and evidentiary uncertainty.
- Scene 1 outcome: material mismatch and transport-log line establish direction, not full proof.
- Sequel turn: Elin chooses immediate off-hours verification rather than waiting for formal process.
- Scene 2 goal: verify night water movement.
- Scene 2 conflict: witness reluctance, risk of exposure, collapsing time window.
- Scene 2 outcome/disaster edge: confirmation of private barge movement, escalating stakes into next chapter.

Pacing is effective on procedural progression, but emotional-weight pacing is uneven. The chapter moves quickly through major human-cost beats (Agnes/Peter, Maud, blood-cough consequences) that could carry more page-time and sensory dwell. Procedural sections are well-developed; key emotional nodes are comparatively compressed.

The single scene break is justified. It marks a real location/time/power shift (priory-controlled day investigation to night quay inquiry under informal risk), and it is not substituting for a missing transition.

### Character Arc Status
Elin advances meaningfully from controlled reconciler to active investigator taking personal risk. Her wound/lie dynamics remain coherent: she still seeks control through lines, custody, and proof, but this chapter shows her starting to prioritize timely witness over institutional patience.

The intended emotional beat (certainty replacing suspicion) is achieved. She moves from inference to converging evidence chains (physical marks, records, witness testimony, route/tide timing), and her final choice posture is more decisive.

Embodiment is present but inconsistent at high-emotion points. There are good touches (touching Peter's blanket, cold climbing from shoes, hands going still), yet several pivotal realizations remain primarily cognitive/procedural when stronger somatic presence would deepen impact.

### World-Building Notes
Continuity and operational logic are strong:
- Receiver control mechanics remain consistent (parallel logs, guarded access, witness language, custody structure).
- Grain-accounting details, barn conditions, threshold wear, tide timing, and quay workflow feel historically grounded and narratively functional.
- Existing continuity threads from Chapters 3-4 are integrated cleanly (Bate, Harwood, source control, attestation-sheet risk, infirmary pressure).

No major rule breaks or plausibility fractures noted.

### Thematic Resonance
Themes of truth versus protective silence, institutional legality versus moral responsibility, and embodied communal cost are clearly active. Christian worldview integration remains natural through liturgical cadence, duty language, and moral choice under pressure rather than sermonizing.

The chapter successfully keeps the human cost in view (Peter, infirmary depletion), but those moments could be expanded slightly to let the moral stakes land more in the body and less in accounting logic alone.

### Bestseller Compliance
- Opening has immediate motion, character function, and tension.
- Chapter ending creates strong forward compulsion.
- Stakes are both personal (Elin's jeopardy, specific sick child) and systemic (active diversion network).
- Emotional beats are present at a good interval, though some high-impact beats need more slowdown to match narrative importance.

Overall compliance is strong, with revision opportunity centered on emotional pacing weight and embodied interiority.

### Strengths
- Procedural investigation is clear, credible, and narratively propulsive.
- Voice matches style guide: Elin's inventory-minded cognition, legal/ecclesial vocabulary, and controlled tension are consistent.
- Dialogue generally serves conflict and information flow without feeling expository-only.
- The reveal ladder is well-built: physical evidence -> documentary anomaly -> witness corroboration -> route confirmation.
- Final line lands chapter promise and urgency effectively.

### Concerns
- Key emotional moments are underweighted relative procedural detail; pacing currently favors method over felt consequence.
- Elin's body disappears in some decisive emotional turns (especially around the transport-log discovery and the moral pivot to immediate action).
- Several dialogue exchanges run heavily informational with limited physical beats in the exchange itself, especially in administrative confrontations.
- Quill interaction is strong on power texture but could carry a sharper embodied threat response from Elin to intensify stakes.

### Suggestions
- Slow 2-3 high-stakes emotional beats by 2-4 sentences each (Agnes/Peter exchange, Maud requisition moment, transport-log realization) with concrete somatic cues tied to Elin's state.
- Add physical beats inside the most procedural dialogue clusters (Quill chapter-house exchange, Tomas bakehouse exchange) so body language and micro-actions carry subtext, not only spoken content.
- In the transition from log discovery to night departure, add a brief reaction-dilemma-decision beat that externalizes internal cost in bodily terms before movement.
- Preserve current procedural density, but trim small redundancies in reporting language where needed to buy space for emotional slowdown without increasing chapter length materially.
- Keep the single scene break; it is doing structural work correctly.
