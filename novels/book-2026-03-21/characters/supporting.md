# Supporting Characters: The Bell of Ash Fen

## Prior Anselm
- **Role**: Prior of Ash Fen Priory; Elin's superior and spiritual counterweight.
- **Voice Note**: Formal, scriptural cadence in public; plain, spare phrasing in private counsel. Uses moral categories before tactical ones.
- **Relationship to Elin (Story Start)**: Trusts her competence and conscience, but urges lawful patience that she experiences as dangerous delay.

## Matthew Cale
- **Role**: Former informer turned reluctant witness-guide; helps locate hidden tally chains.
- **Voice Note**: Quick, defensive, concrete. Speaks in bargains and contingencies; avoids abstract repentance language unless cornered.
- **Relationship to Elin (Story Start)**: She despises and distrusts him. Their alliance begins as transactional necessity and evolves into costly guarded mercy.

## Tomas Reed
- **Role**: Village reeve who challenges Quill's pricing and enforcement claims.
- **Voice Note**: Direct and communal; speaks as "we" more than "I." Uses field and household measures instead of legal terms.
- **Relationship to Elin (Story Start)**: Respects her records skill but resents priory refusal decisions she must deliver under policy.

## Elswith Reed
- **Role**: Tomas's daughter; visible face of household-level hunger stakes.
- **Voice Note**: Blunt, unschooled, emotionally unbuffered. Notices fairness in actions, not explanations.
- **Relationship to Elin (Story Start)**: Sees Elin as someone who might help but often cannot. Her appeals intensify Elin's moral pressure.

## Brother Ivo
- **Role**: Priory cellarer overseeing physical stores, spoilage risk, and ration flow.
- **Voice Note**: Measured and practical; sensory language tied to damp, weight, rot, and handling.
- **Relationship to Elin (Story Start)**: Collaborative professional trust; he supplies material verification for ledger discrepancies.

## Agnes Fenner
- **Role**: Market widow and informal rumor hub in reed-road exchange spaces.
- **Voice Note**: Observant, proverb-rich, socially agile. Embeds warnings in ordinary market talk.
- **Relationship to Elin (Story Start)**: Friendly but wary of priory authority; becomes a key social barometer of village trust.

## Commissioner Halwick
- **Role**: Regional official overseeing formal hearings; not fully aligned with Quill but institutionally cautious.
- **Voice Note**: Legalistic, repetitive, status-conscious. Prioritizes procedural completeness over moral urgency.
- **Relationship to Elin (Story Start)**: Distant authority figure unknown to her personally; later becomes a test of whether truth can survive formal scrutiny.

## Collective Role Map
- **Moral Anchor**: Prior Anselm
- **Compromised Ally / Redemption Vector**: Matthew Cale
- **Community Consequence Lens**: Tomas Reed and Elswith Reed
- **Material Reality Check**: Brother Ivo
- **Social Sentiment Barometer**: Agnes Fenner
- **Institutional Adjudication Pressure**: Commissioner Halwick
