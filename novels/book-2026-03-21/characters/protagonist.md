# Protagonist Sheet: Elin Marris

## Core Identity
- **Name**: Elin Marris
- **Age**: 34
- **Role**: Widowed lay scribe and records keeper at Ash Fen Priory
- **Social Position**: Literate but non-ordained; trusted with books, not power

## Physical Description
- Lean from rationed winters and stair work in granary and bell tower.
- Reed-brown hair usually bound under a plain linen cap; loose strands when under strain.
- Ink-stained fingers, callused thumb from quill pressure and page turning.
- Weather-marked skin with chronic cold-reddened knuckles.
- Habitual stillness in public; movement becomes fast and precise in crisis.

## Ghost, Wound, Lie, Truth Arc

### Ghost (Past Event)
Her husband was executed for refusing a forced oath tied to reform enforcement. She survived by publicly disavowing him in official record, a choice that kept her alive but fractured her identity and moral confidence.

### Wound (Ongoing Damage)
She distrusts her own courage. She experiences survival as proof of compromise, not providence. She overcorrects through procedural perfection, believing any emotional exposure will lead to death or communal collapse.

### Lie (False Belief)
"Silence is the only form of protection. Truth kills the faithful and feeds no one."

### Truth (Hard-Won Belief)
"Truth joined to courage and mercy can protect others, even when it cannot protect me."

### Arc Mechanics (Detailed)
- Early arc: She equates faithfulness with damage control, legal caution, and hidden acts of care.
- Mid arc: Evidence of systemic fraud forces her to see that procedural silence has become active harm.
- Crisis arc: She attempts compromise (false signature) to prevent immediate violence, then faces lethal consequences of that compromise.
- Transformational arc: Confession restores moral agency; she chooses public witness and extends mercy to a compromised ally.
- End state: She no longer defines fidelity as self-preservation, but as truthful stewardship for communal survival.

## Voice Signature

### Sentence Patterns
- Default interior rhythm: ordered, clause-linked, inventory-like sequencing.
- Stress rhythm: compressed syntax, fewer qualifiers, action-forward verbs.
- Emotional deflection: substitutes procedural framing for direct feeling statements.

### Vocabulary and Register
- Practical-literary blend: ledgers, custody, writ, alms, measure, spoilage, transfer, restitution.
- Metaphor pool: balancing accounts, backflooding channels, bell summons, erased names.
- Avoids grand declarations; speaks in consequences and logistics.

### What She Notices First
- Quantities, mismatches, missing seals, changed lock patterns, custody shifts.
- Bodies under strain: hands, breath, hunger posture, queue behavior.
- Power signals embedded in ordinary actions (who holds keys, who signs first, who watches).

## Relationships at Story Start (Chapter 1)
- **Prior Anselm**: Spiritual authority and practical superior. Mutual trust, but strategic tension over risk timing.
- **Rafe Quill**: Not yet physically present in Ch. 1, but known threat category; embodiment of legal force.
- **Village households**: They see her as capable and constrained; she is both helper and gatekeeper.
- **Her husband's memory**: Active internal relationship marked by guilt, silence, and unresolved loyalty.
- **Matthew Cale**: Not yet allied; belongs to her mental ledger under "unforgiven informer class."

## Internal State at Chapter 1
- Cold-focused, sleep-thin, and operating on duty autopilot.
- Carrying anticipatory dread as commissioners approach.
- Believes accuracy can buy survival room, but already suspects food math will fail.
- Spiritually divided: keeps forms of faith while withholding full confession.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Change Summary
- **Ch. 1**: Accepts inventory duty; frames danger as technical workload.
- **Ch. 2**: Ghost is reactivated by oath ledger; recommits to silence-for-survival.
- **Ch. 3**: Meets Quill's control style; feels institutional enclosure.
- **Ch. 4**: Finds sealed contradictory roll; fear now paired with actionable hope.
- **Ch. 5**: Confirms active diversion; suspicion becomes certainty.
- **Ch. 6**: Seeks counsel, hears delay; isolation sharpens.
- **Ch. 7**: Witnesses market suffering; outrage breaches procedural detachment.
- **Ch. 8**: Accepts Matthew's help without trust; moral complexity enters strategy.
- **Ch. 9**: Loses hidden evidence; transitions from careful probing to survival improvisation.
- **Ch. 10**: Sees punitive cost on allies; guilt widens beyond private memory.
- **Ch. 11**: Discovers network-level proof; commits inwardly to public record.
- **Ch. 12**: Faces coercive bargain; fear and pragmatism collide.
- **Ch. 13**: Hides copied evidence; shifts to active countermeasure.
- **Ch. 14**: Signs false schedule under duress; moral nadir.
- **Ch. 15**: Child's death collapses her rationale for compromise.
- **Ch. 16**: Full confession; chooses truth plus mercy as governing principle.
- **Ch. 17**: Rings bell out of order and testifies publicly; accepts personal exposure.
- **Ch. 18**: Protects evidence under fire; courage becomes embodied, not theoretical.
- **Ch. 19**: Defends Matthew from mob revenge; practices mercy under social cost.
- **Ch. 20**: Admits her own perjury in formal hearing; integrity replaces image management.
- **Ch. 21**: Oversees restitution ledger; moves from whistleblower to steward.
- **Ch. 22**: Rewrites communal register with un-erased memory; ends as guardian of truthful continuity.
