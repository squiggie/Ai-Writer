## Developmental Review: Chapter 4 — Internal Report

---

### 1. Scene Goal and Disaster ✅

Caleb's goal is unambiguous from the moment he arrives — he wants the findings documented and acted upon. "I had not called ahead" signals urgency and belief in the system, the refusal to give Sone a chance to prepare a deflection. The disaster lands at appropriate weight. The technical specificity of the wipe (hardware status display, storage medium reporting zero, factory reset signature) earns the horror rather than asserting it. The gap between "forty-two hours after I walked out of Sone's office" and the blank screen is correctly paced.

**Note:** The disaster scene referenced "the original screenshot" on the device. Confirmed consistent with Ch. 1–3: the personal drive is accessed via the home terminal; the wipe targets that partition. The line editor was instructed to reconcile "device" framing with established home terminal architecture.

---

### 2. Sone's Characterization ✅

This is the chapter's strongest structural achievement. Sone is rendered through behavior only: the full rise from the desk, the near-side-of-the-desk seating, the genuine attention, "Take care of yourself. I mean that." These gestures are not undercut — the text does not explain them away as performance. The final line of the meeting section, "I believe he meant it. That is the part I cannot solve," is exactly right. It refuses to resolve the duality and places the irresolvability inside Caleb's consciousness rather than in the narration.

**Watch note (no revision required):** The "rushed" observation — "I filed this observation the way I file system anomalies: noted, uncertain interpretation, do not discard" — is borderline over-exposition. The filing metaphor does the work; the explicit description of the filing process is borderline. If the chapter is revised for any other reason, review whether this can be made one degree more oblique.

---

### 3. The Three Questions ✅

The three questions are structurally sound and correctly sequenced. Question one (configuration change vs. source code modification) is the diagnostic — a specialist's question about invisibility. Question two (current schema vs. legacy tags) tests whether Caleb's evidence has a clean foundation. Question three (bounded window vs. extended pattern) is the trap, and the chapter correctly allows Caleb to walk into it honestly, which is what makes it devastating.

The explicit analysis section — "A man who asks whether null reference suppression requires source code modification… is a man who has read the architecture" — arrives at the correct moment: after the meeting, in Caleb's office, as he writes the questions in the margin. The reader understands this before Caleb states it, because the questions themselves carry sufficient technical precision to signal their meaning.

---

### 4. The 15-Year Parallel ✅

Strong execution. The parallel arrives after the disaster has been discovered and documented, before the final beat — correctly positioned. It is not announced; it arrives as Caleb's mind working through the pattern, consistent with his voice. The specific detail that the original anomaly was a "hardcoded exception that excluded certain behavioral categories from the monitoring threshold" retroactively charges the entire PACEM architecture with a history of intentional suppression, doing thematic work beyond the parallel itself.

"I had been twenty-nine years old and I had wanted to be the kind of engineer who was trusted with larger responsibilities" lands the internal cost of the first capitulation without sentimentality. The parallel is visible to the reader before Caleb names it.

---

### 5. The Paperback / Folded Page ✅

The "unexamined instinct" pattern is consistent with the novel's established instances. The chapter explicitly names the pattern ("The same quality of movement as the night I stayed to run the check a second time… the same quality as saving the screenshot to the personal drive") without over-explaining. The chronological displacement (flashback to the printing three days prior, mid-disaster) is the right structural choice. The detail that Sera gave him the book eight years ago keeps her present without giving her dialogue.

---

### 6. Pacing and Structure ✅

Four clean movements: (1) the entry and access credential detail; (2) the meeting; (3) the wipe discovery; (4) the reckoning. Section breaks manage this correctly. The access credential opening is doing precise work — it establishes Caleb exploiting a known latency gap, which is also the chapter's thematic foreshadowing.

**Observation (no revision required):** The paragraph where Caleb rebuilds the chart and writes Sone's three questions is the chapter's most procedurally dense passage outside the meeting. It is doing necessary work but is most at risk of losing a reader on the emotional track. Could be tightened in a future pass without removing the analysis.

---

Consistent with Chapters 1–3. Short declaratives under stress. Emotion through observation, not statement. "I am aware of finding it effective and I sit down" is precisely right — a person monitoring their own responses in real time. The train-home paragraph is correctly brief and opaque. This is the right amount of Sera.
