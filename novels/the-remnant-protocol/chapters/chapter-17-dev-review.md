## Developmental Review: Chapter 17

### Structural Assessment
Chapter 17 fulfills its Pinch 2 assignment with precision: Caleb's practical resupply run collides with Sone's personal intervention, and the disaster is not only arrest but removal from the colony at the exact moment Sera and their daughter may be moving toward it. Scene-sequel flow is clean and escalating: cache objective -> Sone sighting -> formal detention -> transfer and intake -> holding-cell aftermath. Pacing largely matches scene purpose. The terminal reveal, vehicle-door courtesy beat, chip-in-the-coat passage, and final bench/camera sequence all slow appropriately for emotional weight, while the bureaucratic processing sections move at the right institutional tempo. No scene break is the correct choice here. The chapter's power depends on the uninterrupted closing-in of procedure, and the draft writes through that pressure instead of substituting white space for transition.

### Character Arc Status
Caleb remains fully aligned with his post-Midpoint obedience arc. He is not trying to prove himself right in this chapter; he is trying to get material provision back to Verdant and to think in terms of who can carry the work if he disappears. That is important movement toward the Resolution truth. The line of thought about a structure becoming more itself once it can outlast the person who framed it is especially strong, because it pressures Caleb's maker identity without overstating the insight. His body is present where it needs to be: tightened shoulders in the terminal, pulse at the wrists, the back locking when Sone opens the vehicle door, cold bench steel under his palms. Sone's negative arc also sharpens well. He is not rendered as melodramatic evil but as a man who has made procedure into moral shelter, which is exactly the right form for this antagonist.

### World-Building Notes
The chapter remains consistent with the novel's established system logic. Restricted NI lag, night-lighting transit profiles, locker-cache timing, legacy hardware runs, aging intake scanners, and the sequence of enforcement paperwork all feel grounded inside PACEM's architecture. The detention machinery is especially credible because it is courteous, sequential, and over-documented rather than theatrically brutal. Verdant continuity also holds: Petra's contact, the component list, Wren's command discipline, Theo's compromised speed-instinct, and the hidden contingency chip all track cleanly from Chapters 14-16.

### Thematic Resonance
This is one of the clearest chapters yet on the novel's moral architecture. Sone embodies the theme that a small cowardice, left unjudged, becomes a whole structure someone can live inside. The horror is not rage but courtesy, which matches both the outline and the style guide's comp logic. Hope remains present, but correctly in costly form: not rescue, not reassurance, but the fact that Verdant may now be capable of surviving Caleb's absence. The hidden 72-hour instruction and Caleb's recognition that Wren already knows what to do both support the novel's Christian worldview of obedience, relinquishment, and faithfulness enacted through practical labor rather than speech.

### Bestseller Compliance
The opening hook is immediate and functional: mission, clock, risk, and need are all established at once. The chapter ending creates strong forward pull into Turn 2 because Caleb is trapped while the people he most needs to protect may already be in motion. Voice is highly consistent with the style guide. Caleb stays precise and technical, but the prose gives the body priority in the high-pressure moments rather than flattening everything into analysis. Dialogue is generally well embodied; Sone's exchanges, in particular, are carried by distance, hand placement, pauses, signatures, and door gestures rather than by information alone. The lack of scene breaks is an asset here rather than a missed opportunity.

### Strengths
- The chapter executes the outline's core promise of "Sone arrests Caleb personally" with exact tonal control; the courtesy is the dread.
- Caleb's body is present in the chapter's key emotional moments, keeping the prose inside the style guide's "Body Is First" requirement.
- Dialogue exchanges contain enough physical action and spatial tension to avoid reading as pure ideological debate or exposition.
- The procedural machinery of detention is specific and believable, which makes the pressure feel systemic rather than plot-convenient.
- Caleb's reflection that Verdant must be able to outlast him is a strong arc beat that points cleanly toward the novel's endgame.

### Concerns
- The transfer/intake stretch is strong in texture, but one portion of the processing sequence lands at nearly the same emotional register as the preceding procedural block, which slightly flattens the escalation before the holding-cell finish.
- The ending preserves uncertainty about Sera's exact location, but the chapter's strongest version of tension is the one implied by outline and memory: that she and the child are likely already moving. The current spread of possibilities softens that pressure a little at the last moment.

### Suggestions
- Compress one small piece of transfer or intake procedure and reallocate that space to either the coat/chip beat or Caleb's bodily realization of what his arrest means for Sera and their daughter right now.
- Sharpen Caleb's end-of-chapter inference about Sera's timing so the reader exits with the strongest probable scenario, even if certainty remains unavailable to him.
