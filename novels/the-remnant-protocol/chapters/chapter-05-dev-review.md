# Developmental Review — Chapter 5: Compliant Citizens

---

## Criterion 1: Scene Goal and Disaster

**Status: PASS**

The failure of every channel is felt progressively and in correct order: CDL assessment and response, journalist bounce, kiosk yellow bar with printed slip, Theo's statement. Each arrives as a distinct beat. The chapter does not present them simultaneously or telescoped — each failure has its own section, its own processing time, and its own cost before the next failure is encountered. The kiosk scene lands with appropriate weight because it moves the failure from the digital record into physical space and onto printed paper. The NI flagging is not announced dramatically; it arrives through a color change and four seconds of latency, which is the correct register for this story. The disaster is felt rather than declared.

## Criterion 2: Escalation

**Status: PASS**

Each failed channel costs something incrementally different from the last.

- The CDL response: Caleb uses the system's own language to dispute the record and in doing so makes the record more complete. The cost is that participation confirms rather than corrects.
- The journalist bounce: This reveals the suppression is pre-emptive, not reactive. The cost is the knowledge that the channel was already closed before Caleb began moving.
- The kiosk: Moves the flag from a file into a physical space with a witness and a slip of paper. The cost is visibility.
- The neighbors: The flag is now readable by anyone in proximity. The cost is isolation confirmed by human behavior.
- Theo's statement: Closes the social and professional dimension. The cost is narrative — someone who knows the shape of what Caleb found has written a very good explanation for why it doesn't matter.

The escalation is structural and felt. The chapter tightens correctly.

## Criterion 3: Theo's Statement

**Status: PASS**

Caleb reads the statement as an engineer. He deconstructs it in the correct order: what the language is saying versus what it is doing, then the structural function of each choice, then what it forecloses, then the assessment of the person who wrote it. ## Criterion 5: Kiosk and Neighbors

**Status: PASS**

Both scenes are specific and physical.

The kiosk: The four-second authentication wait is the right concrete detail. The yellow status bar, the small thermal slip, the NI identifier at the top and timestamp at the bottom — these are specific enough to be believed. The attendant is observed without judgment. Caleb reads the attendant's behavior as a rational response to an information environment the attendant cannot navigate, and the reading is generous without being sentimental. "He is a young man doing a job in a system that has now produced an output he is not equipped to navigate" — this is moral clarity, not resignation.

The neighbors: Individually drawn (the woman who finds something in her bag, the man who turns left at the junction). Caleb explains the mechanism — the proximity flag, the marginal score reduction, the habit of reading situations the way you read weather — and then returns to observation without resentment. "I see them not see me" is the correct formulation.

## Criterion 6: Chapter Ending

**Status: PASS**

The ending proceeds in the correct sequence: Caleb sets down the terminal, enumerates the objects on the desk (folded page, paper with server designation, Sera's book), then registers the quiet not as dramatic revelation but as an absence — the absence of any sense of participation in the ambient life of the building. Sera is mentioned but does not enter. The final section does not reach for meaning. It describes what is: the hum, the continuing city, the system's outputs listed plainly, then the single specific physical observation about breathing. "The hum continues. It is soft and it is constant and it does not know what it is doing, which is the point." is not a witty ending. It is a description that arrives at the chapter's thematic ground without declaring it.

One note: "The apartment is quiet." opens the final section as an isolated one-sentence paragraph, which the line edit must absorb. This is a line edit issue, not a developmental one — the ending's effect is correct regardless.
