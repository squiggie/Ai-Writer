# Developmental Review — Chapter 7: Wren

## Overall Status: APPROVED

The chapter is structurally sound and thematically on target. All eight criteria are met. Six specific line-level corrections are required before final; they are documented under **Corrections Required** below and carried into the line edit.

## Criterion-by-Criterion Assessment

### 1. Wren's First Appearance

**APPROVED.**

"You're about seven minutes ahead of where I thought you'd be. The bike?" establishes her register immediately and correctly. She is crouched in the soil, hands in the earth, not looking up when she speaks — no welcome, no coldness, simply a data point and a confirming question. The approach to the door, the gaze when she finally looks (specification being confirmed against its description), the instruction to come inside when ready — all precise. The prose gloss on her voice ("not the studied evenness of someone performing calm, but the actual flatness of someone for whom this moment is not a surprise") earns its place; it does the work of orienting the reader without sentimentalizing the beat. She arrives unhurried and calibrated. Correct first appearance.

### 2. The Briefing / Confirmation

**APPROVED.**

Her single question — "When did the null references start clustering in the healthcare allocation category?" — does exactly the right work: it tells us she already knows the shape of the pattern and needs one temporal coordinate to lock her own data against his. Caleb's answer then makes "That's when they started flagging my patients" land as confirmation rather than discovery. The clinical account that follows (three filings, the compliance record generated against her license, the suspension, three patients dead in the window) is delivered without bitterness, as intake data. The precision of "I had filed correctly. I had used the right channels. I was not fast enough." is the chapter's emotional hinge and it is handled correctly — stated plainly, no elaboration, Wren not looking for a response.

One passage requires a line correction: "It is like watching a load-bearing calculation resolve" violates Line Rule 2 (no "load-bearing" as metaphor). See Corrections Required.

### 3. The Facility Tour

**APPROVED.**

The tour reads as rounds, not a showcase. She checks the header tank level as she passes because that is what she does, not because she is showing Caleb the water system. Each element is presented as a maintenance status or operational fact: the pump service window overdue by three months, the valve seal she cannot source, the three generations of germination trial data she presents without calling them an accomplishment, the intercom she built and the interference problem she diagnosed by differential exclusion. The prose summary of "three years of choices made concrete in soil tests and water lines and a copper-wire intercom she wired by hand" completes the section correctly — it is Caleb's synthesis of what he has observed, not authorial praise.

### 4. Wren's Voice

**APPROVED.**

All of her spoken lines are short, plain, and complete. The longest spoken passage is her clinical account of the three filings — appropriate in length because it is testimony, not explanation, and because it ends before it qualifies. No line runs long unnecessarily. Her metaphors and reference points are drawn from the body and from living things: hands in soil, germination rates, root depth, differential diagnosis. "Where else would I go" (not a question) is correct — it closes without inviting response. "All right. Then you know the price of today." is correct — two sentences, the second doing the work of acknowledging what Caleb already knows without restating it. No warmth performed, no indifference performed.

### 5. Emotional Register

**APPROVED.**

Relief without softness. The chapter does not soften into warmth or hope. The meal is plain — root vegetables, preserved greens, bread — and has no ceremony. Her arithmetic on Caleb's NI situation is done in silence; when she speaks it is to confirm that he already knows the cost. The prose does not editorialize the significance of this first alignment. Caleb's registration of it is in the specificity of what he observes, not in any emotional commentary. The register holds throughout.

### 6. Wren's Exit Line

**APPROVED.**

"You found the thing. Now you have to decide if you're building something or just complaining loudly." The surrounding prose ("Not unkindly. The way you give a patient the name of what they have, when the name is accurate and the patient is capable of hearing it and the most useful thing you can do is say it plainly.") correctly characterizes the delivery without softening or dramatizing it. The close is clean: she goes inside, the heavy door closes completely and without drama. No lingering, no callback.

### 7. The Ride Back

**APPROVED with correction required.**

The question is held open correctly. Caleb turns it in his hands rather than resolving it: "I do not know if I am building something. I know what I have found. I know the shape of her question. I know what it requires, and I do not know yet if I am the person who can meet it." The final image — Vael ahead, atmospheric boundary announcing itself, the question still carried — is correct. The very last sentence, "I keep riding," is an isolated one-sentence paragraph in violation of Line Rule 1. It must be absorbed into the paragraph above. See Corrections Required.

### 8. Voice Consistency / Continuity

**APPROVED.**

Caleb's voice is consistent with prior chapters: first-person, systems vocabulary, deliberate accumulation of detail, no emotional shorthand. The NI sync cost is established at the chapter's opening ("approximately sixty-one hours remaining," and "I have been counting it since I left Vael," and "I went anyway") and realized on the ride back ("The NI has already missed its sync window. Somewhere in the CDL a flag has been generated, a missed-authentication event attached to my account"). The callback is structurally clean. Wren debuts in her correct role — not a helper, not an ally in the conventional sense, a person already doing the work who now has the documentation she was missing.
