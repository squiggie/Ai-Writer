# Developmental Review — Chapter 6: The Closed Channels

## Criterion 1: Compressed Time Structure / Case-File Voice

APPROVED.

The chapter opens on a present-state tableau (the rebuilt chart), establishes the two-week frame immediately, then moves through each channel failure in diagnostic register before dropping into the Sera scene as present-moment action. The case-file voice is consistent and controlled throughout: "I note the time," "I mark this branch closed," "The three-hour duration is data." These are appropriate diagnostic markers that register as Caleb's mode of thought rather than narrative convenience.

The transition into the Sera scene works. "Sera works late on Thursdays" reorients to behavioral observation without announcing a mode shift. The reader arrives in the present moment without a seam.

One line is flagged for line edit: "This is the condition of my days." functions as a summary declaration closing the opening section. As a standalone paragraph it reads as a punchy one-liner rather than integrated prose. It must be absorbed into adjacent material.

## Criterion 2: Channel Failures — Escalation and Cost

APPROVED.

The four channels fail with distinct texture:

- **CDL forum:** Active engagement; Caleb generates compliance records and teaches the model. The cost is both the compliance records (four total, weaponized against him) and the realization that his attempts are feeding the system information.
- **Physical post:** He sends knowing it will fail. The cost here is different — it is the act of sitting with a foregone conclusion, of sending anyway because not sending produces less. The NI metadata paragraph is the sharpest statement of structural entrapment in the chapter.
- **Academic contacts:** Faster, more mechanical rejection. The form reads his NI before he finishes entering his name. Cost is the speed of erasure — no engagement at all.
- **Radio/notice board:** Archaeological. The channels were closed before he arrived. Cost is the discovery that he was not late to find a door — the door was never there.

The sequence tightens rather than listing. Each failure costs something distinct. The escalation is functional.

## Criterion 3: CDL Timing Detail (3h18m → 2h4m → 47m)

APPROVED.

The three removal windows are presented as data Caleb is generating, not as evidence of his victimization. He reads the first window as information about queue cycles. He recognizes the contraction as the model learning from his attempts. The explicit statement — "I am not discovering a gap in the system. I am teaching the system where to look" — is the correct diagnostic posture. The numbers function as the chapter's sharpest piece of diagnostic irony: his own methodology is the instrument of his containment.

## Criterion 4: Caleb's Posture — Working the Problem

APPROVED.

No despair. No collapse. He checks the notice board three times "because I want to be wrong." He sends the letters after reasoning through their futility, because "not sending them does not produce a different outcome. It only produces an outcome with less in it." The channel survey ends with a structural diagnosis, not an emotional one — the paragraph on information topology is Caleb's analytical intelligence working at full capacity on material that is actively foreclosing his options. This is the correct posture: methodical exhaustion of possibility, not surrender.

## Criterion 5: Sera's Scene — Clinical Register

APPROVED.

Sera holds the register without deviation. She reads the notice in clinical vocabulary. She sits "the way hands fold when she is in a review session." She does not accuse, explain, or break. The cost surfaces only in precise, small observations: the particular quality of quiet when she enters; the pause before the cabinet; Caleb's note that her eyes carry fatigue beyond what fatigue alone accounts for. The register is working to carry the weight of what she is not naming — and the draft makes this visible without stating it. The final description of her hands and posture is exact without being clinical to the point of coldness.

One line flagged for line edit: "She reads it out." (standalone paragraph) must be absorbed into the surrounding prose.

## Criterion 6: Chapter Ending

APPROVED.

The chapter ends with two objects on the table — chart and printout — and holds there. No resolution, no confrontation, no melodrama. The final paragraph places the city continuing outside the window as counterpoint and closes on the printout's specificity: "gives the date exactly." This is evidence next to evidence. The ending earns its stillness.
