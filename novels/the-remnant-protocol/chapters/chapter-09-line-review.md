## Issues Found

### Rule 1 — No Isolated One-Sentence Paragraphs

**One instance flagged.**

> *Thank you. I had not heard that particular sign-off before, in the documentation I worked on.*

The sentence "Thank you." standing alone as a paragraph-fragment creates a false dramatic beat. The two sentences should be folded into the preceding paragraph, attached to the notification text itself. The pacing works better as part of the observation about the message than as a standalone punctuation moment.

**Fix:** Attach to the paragraph ending with "the message ends."

### Rule 5 — No Assistant-Language Bleed

One instance flagged:

> *the thing I helped design is now speaking to me in the voice we calibrated for compliance, and it is being precisely, professionally polite about the fact that I cannot eat breakfast without going somewhere first.*

The phrase "precisely, professionally polite" is not assistant-language exactly, but the construction — with its three-p alliteration — reads as editorial performance rather than Caleb's voice. The surrounding prose is doing the work already; this sentence is summarizing what the reader has absorbed. Cut or flatten. Proposed: remove the sentence entirely. The paragraph's meaning is complete without it; the next paragraph ("I look at the transit corridor below") lands cleaner.

### Rule 6 — Final Section Sentence Length (last 400 words)

The final section (from "The apartment is exactly as I left it" onward) is broadly correct in register. Four specific flags:

**Flag A:**
> *Three months of this. Before the three months: fifteen years of the work I did and the report I buried and the anomaly I let Sone tell me was noise. After three months: four compliance records. No institutional affiliation. The 7-F flag, which marks me in every system query in Vael as a person the system is tracking. Sera's things are gone. The entry lock takes two cycles. I am on Level Three provisioning, which means every kiosk in the city now shows a red indicator before I have touched the screen.*

The structure is correct: short declarative sentences under emotional load. However, the sentence "The 7-F flag, which marks me in every system query in Vael as a person the system is tracking" runs eight words longer than it needs to. "The 7-F flag marks me in every system query in Vael" is sufficient. The subordinate clause is doing what the main clause already does.

**Flag B:**
> *I have sent the documentation to six people who might have acted on it. I have been told three times that my findings are unverified and my conduct since the reclassification has generated concerns about my professional judgment. I have been told once that the organization I contacted has no capacity to take on new cases of this type. I have not been told by the remaining two, because they have not responded.*

This paragraph is accurate but runs slightly long for where the chapter is emotionally. The last sentence ("I have not been told by the remaining two, because they have not responded") explains what silence means. Caleb does not need to explain it. Cut the sentence. The reader understands that two non-responses are non-responses. The silence is part of the point.

**Flag C:**
> *I found Wren, who is one person in a building forty minutes outside Vael with a water system and a seed store and three years of working alone.*

The phrase "and three years of working alone" is doing work the prior chapters have already done. Trim to: "a water system and a seed store." Wren's three years stand on their own in the reader's memory.

**Flag D:**
> *I have been trying to be believed for three months.*
> *I have not been believed by anyone who could act on it.*

These two sentences are the correct plainness. No change needed. The rhythm here is the chapter at its best.

### Rule 7 — Word Repetition (3+ in 200 words)

**"Documentation" — 4 instances in final section (within approximately 250 words):**
- "I have sent the documentation to six people"
- "I have the documentation"
- "The documentation I worked on" (opening)
- "the CDL access" section: "the terminal documentation"

The final section uses "documentation" three times in close proximity. The word is correct and is Caleb's vocabulary, but the density reads as repetition. Suggested replacements for two of the three final-section instances:
- "I have sent the documentation to six people" — keep (this is the operative word here).
- "I have the evidence. I have the cases. I have everything I had three months ago" — replace "I have the documentation" with "I have the evidence" or restructure as below.
- "the terminal documentation" in the operational section is fine at its distance from the final section.

**"Chart" — 5 instances total; 3 in final section.** Not a problem: the chart is a physical object being actively referenced each time. No change needed.

**"Provisioning" — 4 instances.** Functional, not decorative. No change needed.

### Prose Rhythm

The chapter's rhythm is strongest in the compliance office scene and in the opening. The middle walking section has two passages where the sentence structure becomes compound-heavy in a way that slows the walk. Flag:

> *I know something else about bandwidth assessment, which is that you can be completely accurate about your current threat level and still not account for the moment when the queue processes.*

This can be broken into two sentences for clarity and rhythm:
"I know something else about bandwidth assessment. You can be completely accurate about your current threat level and still not account for the moment when the queue processes."

The current single-sentence version is not wrong, but the period between the two clauses would let the second land harder.
