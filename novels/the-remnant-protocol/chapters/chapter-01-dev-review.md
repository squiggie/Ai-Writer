### Concerns

- **Sone introduction (outline vs. draft):** The outline lists "Director Aldric Sone (in flashback)" as a character present in Chapter 1. Sone appears in the flashback, but only as a past memory — Caleb recalls him commissioning the audit layer, and thinks about him in the context of the discovery ("I thought about Sone"). This is functionally appropriate for Chapter 1, but the outline describes Sone as being actively present in the flashback in a way the chapter does not deliver. The outline's scene structure note says the flashback should cover "Caleb at career peak (the work he's proud of, the audit layer he built, the genuine competence that made him trust the system)." The chapter does this. Sone's role as commissioner of the audit layer is included. This is minor but flagging for tracking: if the outline intends a direct Sone flashback scene (dialogue, interaction), it is not present. If the outline intends Sone as a presence-in-mind only, it is present. Clarify for Chapter 1 final.

- **Healthcare triage case specificity:** The null reference anomaly is anchored to a specific case: a citizen in Zone 14 with a cardiac irregularity whose expedited care request was denied. The bible establishes that PACEM's override events cluster around individuals flagged for "independent resource acquisition" (Ch. 3 outline). A healthcare denial for a cardiac case does not obviously fit that pattern. This is not a contradiction — PACEM processes nine hundred healthcare triage denials per day, and the specific entry Caleb finds is presented as an ordinary-seeming category — but a reader who later reviews Chapter 1 in light of Chapter 3's revelation may note the disconnect. This could be intentional (the full pattern is not visible from a single entry) or an inconsistency worth addressing when the Chapter 3 draft is reviewed. Flag for continuity check at Ch. 3.

- **"Three weeks earlier" timeline vs. chapter metadata:** The chapter states the flashback is "three weeks earlier," but the chapter header places the flashback at "2083 minus 3 days." Three weeks and three days are not the same. One of these is a metadata error. Recommend reconciling. (The prose's "three weeks" is more dramatically appropriate given the outline's description of events in "late October" for the flashback and "Day 1" for the present.)

- **Ghost/wound backstory absence is correct, but the chapter does not have a placeholder beat for it:** The character file establishes that 15 years before the story Caleb buried an anomaly at Sone's direction. This backstory is not present in Chapter 1, which is appropriate. However, there is no hint or trace of it either — no moment where Caleb's certainty about the audit layer has a slight catch, no submerged texture that would allow a reader on re-read to notice what was always there. This is a re-read layer issue, not a first-read issue. It is low-priority for draft but worth considering in revision: a single sentence in the flashback where Caleb's confidence has a grain of something he does not examine would pay dividends on a second reading.
