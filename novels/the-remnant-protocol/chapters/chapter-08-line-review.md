## Specific Changes

### Change 1 — Analytical explanation in daughter section

**Location:** Paragraph beginning "She is performing the gravity she can feel is required..."

**Draft text:**
"She is performing the gravity she can feel is required, which is how children manage the distance between what they know is happening and what they can hold in their mind at one time."

**Issue:** The clause beginning "which is how children manage" steps outside Caleb's observational register into interpretive explanation. Caleb is observing his daughter. He should not be taxonomizing child psychology. The observation itself ("performing the gravity she can feel is required") is strong and should stand. The explanatory clause weakens it by closing the gap the reader should be left to cross.

**Revised text:**
"She is performing the gravity she can feel is required."

**Effect:** The sentence stands alone as observation. The reader understands. The gap between her age and the solemnity she is producing is left open, which is correct.
